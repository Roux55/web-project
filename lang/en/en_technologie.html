A. Towards task automation

Algorithms make us evolve technologically, i.e. to acquire knowledge and also to produce but also discover more (and especially faster).

Indeed, algorithms then allow machines to produce more efficiently than a human being. As a result, we can see many machines in these factories with the aim of mass production in a fixed and constant way, which a human being could not do. However, all this creates an anxiety and fear among the employees, who performed these tasks manually, of losing their work. This is why in the world of technology, algorithms are becoming more and more important.


However, there are many other cases in which algorithms are very important, simplifiers or even indispensable. For example, mass production (with the hybrid flow shopOrganising one's time and optimising one's resources) interacting in a world with increased or virtual reality, implementing an artificial intelligence, advising a user, and even making estimates.

B. Architectural use

Algorithms are also used in the building or construction sector, but this time they are not detrimental to the employee. Indeed, the latter will be assisted by robots in their tasks, for example in the modelling of future buildings or in the realisation and plan calculations. These are also widely used in the agricultural sector to plan automatic watering and even automated harvesting by robots.


To conclude this part, we can clearly see that society has evolved, is evolving and will evolve thanks to algorithms in any field.
